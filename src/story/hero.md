# Hi, I'm Becky.

I'm a senior product manager who got into product because I taught myself SQL.

My degree is in marketing. I started in market research, then took a business analyst job at a credit union, where I picked up SQL and Tableau because the questions I wanted to answer kept outrunning the tools I was given. A recruiter noticed and pulled me into product. I joined [Octane11](https://octane11.com), a B2B data and AI startup, as the second product hire, and I've been promoted twice since: product ops, then product manager, then senior product manager.

Today I own our AI products. I shipped our AI chat, which sits on a homegrown MCP server over our customers' data and turns any question into the right dashboard, already filtered. Now I'm building our first agent, which is learning to do campaign mapping, one of the core jobs of the platform, on its own.

Nobody handed me any of those steps. I learned the skill first, built something real with it, and the title showed up afterward. It's the only career strategy I've ever had, and at night I run the same loop on products of my own.
